About me

Just to give a bit of background

I’m Sam - a computer science student hoping to some day help provide gamers with unforgettable gaming experiences similar to the ones I’ve had over my years of gaming.

My history

I was introduced into the world of writing code back in secondary school. An email was passed around my year group offering a select number of students to be introduced onto a ‘Computing’ module rather than the standard ‘ICT’ module which all other students sat.

On this Computing module, we was introduced to the scripting language Python. It took my interest immediately; I then knew it was an ambition of mine to become a programmer.

Once I knew what career path I wanted to go down the rest of my academic choices came with ease - in 6th form I took the BTEC IT course. Students who didn’t sit a BTEC module had to choose 3 different AS level modules instead. This wasn’t appealing to me, I wanted to focus all of my knowledge into the world of IT.

At 6th form I gained a vast amount of knowledge about computers in general, but not so much about programming. It wasn’t until my final year at 6th form that we sat a game design module. Other students in my class used the software ‘GameMaker: Studio’ but I prefered the more challenging root because I knew it would help my knowledge a lot more in the long-run if I wanted to make a career out of game development. I used Unity. It was hard. I loved it. Even something as simple as making a 3D car move around a terrain made me extremely happy and eager to learn more. I knew from before I wanted to become a programmer but now my career path had been narrowed down further, I wanted to use my programming skills to develop games.

After 6th form, university was the most obvious choice for me. Unsurprisingly, I chose the Computer Science with Games Development course. Although it was challenging, university taught me an incredible amount and I’d always recommend it to anybody with the same desire to learn that I have.

C# was the ‘main’ lanugage taught for our year at university and it became my strongest programming language by far; emphasis on the word main. I was also taught a series of other languages such as C++, OpenGL, OpenTK, Javascript and even Prolog.

Because C# is my strongest lanugage, using Unity would be the most logical game development tool for me to learn due to its C# support which other tools such as Unreal Engine doesn’t have. Although some day I may dabble with Unreal, we’ll see.

And that’s where I leave you, Enjoy the blog :)
